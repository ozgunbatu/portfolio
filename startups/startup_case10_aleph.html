Strategic Recommendations

1. Vertical-Specific Models

Rationale: Generic LLM = commoditized. Build domain-specific models (legal, medical, manufacturing).

  • Legal AI: trained on German law (BGB, StGB), case law
  • Medical AI: GDPR-compliant patient data, German healthcare
  • Premium pricing: 3-5x general model

Impact: Defensible niches vs OpenAI

2. Open Source Community Play

Rationale: Can't outspend OpenAI. Release Luminous-Base (13B params) as open source.

  • Build developer community (like Mistral AI)
  • Upsell to enterprise on-premise licenses
  • Crowdsource improvements via fine-tuning

Impact: Developer mindshare, faster iteration

3. EU Government Consortium

Rationale: Lobby for ‚Ç¨1B EU fund for sovereign AI (like GAIA-X for cloud).

  • Position Aleph Alpha as "European champion"
  • Guaranteed government contracts = revenue floor
  • Matching funds for compute infrastructure

Impact: ‚Ç¨500M+ guaranteed revenue, compute parity

Takeaway: Aleph Alpha's "sovereign AI" thesis credible for regulated industries (defense, finance, healthcare) where data residency + explainability = hard requirements. However, general enterprise market (80% TAM) choosing 10x cheaper OpenAI unless EU regulations force switch. Competitive moats: (1) EU AI Act compliance (explainability), (2) On-premise deployment (air-gapped), (3) German government relationships. Key risks: (1) Compute gap widens (can't keep up with GPT-5), (2) Microsoft offers EU residency (removes sovereignty wedge), (3) Funding insufficient for next model generation. Path forward: vertical specialization (legal, medical), open source base model, lobby for EU subsidies.
